Samsung, a multinational conglomerate headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, operates in diverse sectors including consumer electronics,
semiconductors, telecommunications, and home appliances. Known for its innovation and heavy investment in research and development, Samsung is a leading producer of smartphones,
notably the Galaxy series, and dominates the semiconductor industry with its memory chips. Founded in 1938 by Lee Byung-chul, the family-owned conglomerate has expanded globally,
maintaining a significant presence in various industries. Despite facing legal challenges and patent disputes, Samsung remains one of the world's most recognizable and valuable brands,
committed to corporate social responsibility and environmental sustainability. Its diversification into areas such as
shipbuilding, construction, and insurance ensures its stability and mitigates economic risks.

Samsung, founded in 1938 and now led by Lee Jae-yong, has evolved from a trading company into a global conglomerate with annual revenues exceeding $200 billion.
Known for technological milestones like the first digital and 3D TVs and pioneering foldable smartphones, Samsung employs over 300,000 people worldwide.
Its subsidiaries include Samsung Heavy Industries, a leading shipbuilder, and Samsung Display, a major OLED and LCD screen manufacturer.
The company is committed to environmental initiatives and invests heavily in AI and IoT. Samsung also supports major sports events and teams, enhancing its global brand visibility.
Additionally, the Samsung Medical Center in Seoul is a top medical research and treatment facility in Asia.
